This dataset is designed for the purpose of detecting and classifying engine faults using advanced signal processing and machine learning techniques. It contains data representing sensor readings collected during various operating conditions of an engine, including normal, minor fault, and critical fault states.

Features:
The dataset includes 11 independent variables (sensor readings and derived features) and 1 dependent variable (engine condition label). Below is a detailed description of the columns:

Column Name Description Unit Range
Vibration_Amplitude Peak vibration amplitude measured in the engine mm/s² 0.1 - 10.0
RMS_Vibration Root Mean Square (RMS) of engine vibration mm/s² 0.05 - 5.0
Vibration_Frequency Frequency of engine vibration Hz 20 - 2000
Surface_Temperature Temperature of the engine surface °C 30 - 150
Exhaust_Temperature Temperature of the exhaust gas °C 200 - 600
Acoustic_dB Acoustic noise level generated by the engine dB 60 - 120
Acoustic_Frequency Acoustic signal frequency of the engine Hz 100 - 5000
Intake_Pressure Intake manifold pressure kPa 90 - 120
Exhaust_Pressure Exhaust gas pressure kPa 80 - 110
Frequency_Band_Energy Energy of the signal in a specific frequency band (from Short-Time Fourier Transform) Arbitrary Units 0.1 - 1.0
Amplitude_Mean Average signal amplitude over specific time windows Arbitrary Units 0.01 - 0.5
Engine_Condition Label indicating the engine’s operating condition: - 0 (Normal), 1 (Minor Fault), 2 (Critical Fault)
Data Characteristics:
Total Samples: 10,000
Class Distribution:
60% Normal (0): Represents the engine operating without any faults.
30% Minor Fault (1): Represents the engine operating with minor issues.
10% Critical Fault (2): Represents the engine operating under severe fault conditions.
